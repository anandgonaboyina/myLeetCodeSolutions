You need find the <strong>Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)</strong> of the two given numbers. Return the <strong>GCD </strong>of the two numbers.</p><p><br></p><p>The <strong>Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)</strong> of two integers is the <strong>largest </strong>positive integer that <strong>divides </strong>both of the integers.</p><br><br><p class="tuf-text-14">Example 1</p><div class="tuf-example text-new_secondary"><p><strong>Input</strong>: n1 = 4, n2 = 6</p><p><strong>Output</strong>: 2</p><p><strong>Explanation</strong>: Divisors of n1 = 1, 2, 4, Divisors of n2 = 1, 2, 3, 6</p><p>Greatest Common divisor = 2.</p></div><br><br><p class="tuf-text-14 ">Example 2</p><div class="tuf-example text-new_secondary"><p><strong>Input</strong>: n1 = 9, n2 = 8</p><p><strong>Output</strong>: 1</p><p><strong>Explanation</strong>: Divisors of n1 = 1, 3, 9 Divisors of n2 = 1, 2, 4, 8.</p><p>Greatest Common divisor = 1.</p></div><br><br><p class="tuf-text-14 ">Constraints</p><div class="tuf-dark-content-box"><ul><li>1 &lt;= n1, n2 &lt;= 1000</li></ul></div><br><br>
